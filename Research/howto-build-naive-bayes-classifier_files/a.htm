  • Dean Jones

    It's worth noting that stemming is not the same as lemmatisation. The aim of stemming isn't to produce the "morphological root" (i.e. the lemma) of a word; this is what lemmatisation is for. The aim of stemming is to generate a common stem for morphologically-related words, but that stem is not necessarily the lemma.

  • Alex N.

    Not exactly. For instance, if one word appears only in one spam document (like "money"), then its basic probability will be 0 for ham. But this is not necessarily the case, being more of an artifact of our small dataset . So that function counteracts this effect. The assumed probability of 0.5 is a good default.

    So that's the purpose of that function. In case you've got a small data-set, to counteract the effects of rare words.
